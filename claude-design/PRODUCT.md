# Bond — Product Snapshot

## In one line
A reminder app that helps couples express the five love languages on cue — words, acts, gifts, time, touch.

## Who it's for
- Couples in established relationships (not dating-app territory).
- Long-distance, new parents, busy professionals — anyone whose partner is feeling under-prioritized.
- iPhone-first, with Apple Watch dictation and lock-screen widgets as system polish.
- Also usable solo (journaling / self-care reminders).

## Core loop
1. Pair with partner (one generates code, one accepts via universal link).
2. Create reminders for self or partner. Tag with a love language. Pick when (one-time / recurring / location / random window).
3. The reminder fires as a notification. The recipient (you or them) takes the small action.
4. Tap "Done" to log it. Over time the app shows your love-language balance, streak, and trends.

## Tabs
1. **Reminders** — the main list.
2. **Check-In** — one shared daily question with reveal-after-both-answer dynamic. *(premium)*
3. **Milestones** — anniversaries / birthdays with countdown widget. *(premium)*
4. **Insights** — love language balance, streaks, trends. *(premium)*

## Free vs Premium
- **Free:** Unlimited reminders (one-time + recurring), pairing, widgets.
- **Premium:** Location-triggered reminders, random-window "surprise" reminders, Milestones, Insights, Daily Check-In, Templates.

## Tone of voice
- Warm but not saccharine.
- Adult, not couples-app-cute. No "💕" in product UI.
- Specific over abstract: "Tell her you noticed she stayed up late finishing the project" beats "Show affection."
- Quiet authority. The app doesn't pep-talk the user — it just gives a small, well-timed prompt.

## What it deliberately is not
- Not a messenger. Reminders are nudges, not chat.
- Not a habit tracker. Streaks exist but the framing is "tending a relationship" not "don't break the chain."
- Not gamified. No XP, no badges, no leaderboards.
- Not a journal. Notes on reminders are short and contextual, not freeform diary.
- Not a tool for new relationships. The marketing assumes monogamous, established couples.
